A Brief Description of our Precious Stones

Amethyst

Amethysts are a violet variety of quartz. Comes from the Greek name Koine. It's violet color comes from irridation, impurities of iron and some transition metals resulting in complex crystal lattice substitutions. High quality Amethysts may be found in Siberia, Sri Lanka, Brazil, Uruguay and in the far East.

Emerald

Emeralds are very precious stones, and may be found all over the world, but especially in North Africa, specifically in Egypt. May also be found all over the world, such as Afghanistan, Australia, Austria, Brazil, Bulgaria, Canada, China, Ethiopia, Russia, South Africa, the U.S, Zambia and Zimbabwe.

Ruby

A pinkish red to blood-red colored gemstone, is one of the most popular jewelery gems and very durable. Rubies are cardinal gems, like amethysts, sapphires, emeralds and diamonds. Rubies are mostly mined in Myanmar and Thailand, but may also be found in Cambodia, Afghanistan, Australia, Brazil, Colombia, India, Namibia, Japan, Madagascar, Mozambique, Nepal, Pakistan, Tanzania and Vietnam.

Sapphire

A precious gem stone, a variety of the mineral corundum containing aluminum oxide with traces of iron, titanium, chronium, vanadium or magnesium. Comes from the Greek "sapphirus" reffering to Lapis Lazuli. Significant deposits may be found in Australia, Afghanistan, Cambodia, Cameroon, China, Colombia, Ethiopia, Kashmir, Kenya, Laos, Madagascar, Malawi, Mozambique, Myanmar, Nigeria, Ruanda, Sri Lanka, Tanzania, Thailand and the U.S.

Aquamarine

Aquamarine is a pale-blue to light-green variety of beryl. The color can be changed by heat. It is a very common gem stone, and contains no or few noticeable inclusions. The name comes from aqua, latin for "water" and marine deriving from marina, latin for "of the sea". Is inexpensive due to its abundance, and ancient Romans believed that Aquamarine could protect people who are travelling across the sea, and to prevent illnesses.

Topaz

Topaz in its natural state is colorless, though trace elements impurities can make it pale blue or golden brown to yellow orange. Topaz is mainly treated with heat or radiation to make it a deep-blue, reddish-orange, pale green, pink or purple. The rarest are natural pinks, reds, and delicate golden orange sometimes with pink hues.

Diamonds

Diamond is a solid form of the carbon element, with its atoms arranged in a crystal structure called diamond cubic. Diamonds have the highest hardness and thermal conductivity of any natural material. Most natural diamonds have ages between 1 billion and 3.5 billion years, formed at depths between 150 and 250 kilometers in the earth's mantle. May be cut in several shapes, such as round, pear, marquise, oval, hearts and arrows.

Brown Diamonds

Also considered "chocolate" diamonds, they are the most common color variety of natural diamonds. There are several causes for the brown color, including irridiation treatment, nickel impurities and lattice defects associated with plastic deformation. Other diamond colors are champagne, amber, cognac, and some companies named different shades such as clove, coffee, caramel, cappuccino, mocha, espresso, cinnamon and even tobacco.
